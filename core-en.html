What Core is

The deterministic nucleus behind GEENESSYS.

GEENESSYS 7.1.1. concentrates the planning, coordination, and execution capabilities that power physical and digital solutions across the ecosystem. Its goal is to solve critical decisions through structured logic, not probabilistic improvisation.

3D/4D engine

Represents routes, obstacles, and decisions in space and time for dynamic scenarios.

Multi-agent coordination

Designed for fleets, swarms, robots, parallel processes, and distributed systems.

Auditable execution

Produces traceable decisions and events for technical review, validation, and iterative improvement.

Operating model

One core. Two deployment modes.

GEENESSYS 7.1.1. can live close to the physical agent or operate as an orchestration layer. This enables a transition from an autonomous node to a distributed operation without changing the core logic.

Components

Designed to integrate, not to lock clients in.

GEENESSYS 7.1.1. can be evaluated as a library, SDK, on-prem server, or integrated component inside an existing architecture. The modality depends on the environment, hardware, the level of control required, and the specific use case.

Edge

Lightweight instance designed to operate close to a drone, robot, sensor, industrial endpoint, or physical node.

  • Local operation
  • Low-latency response
  • Reduced network dependency

Omni

Orchestration instance to coordinate agents, consolidate telemetry, and distribute missions.

  • Central/local coordination
  • Aggregated telemetry
  • Multi-agent operation

SDK

Technical integration for teams that need to embed the engine within existing systems.

  • Progressive integration
  • Technical access under NDA
  • Controlled testing

On-prem server

On-premise deployment for clients that require control over data, execution, and operations.

  • Operational control
  • Data within your own infrastructure
  • Enterprise or industrial integration
Decision flow

From input to executable command.

GEENESSYS 7.1.1. operates within a controlled chain: it receives structured information, evaluates constraints, decides through deterministic logic, and outputs traceable results.

01: INPUT

The system receives operational context: position, routes, obstacles, constraints, environment, mission rules, or process data.

02: EVALUATION

Core evaluates geometry, time, constraints, and agent state to determine a viable route, action, or decision.

03: EXECUTION

The decision becomes commands, routes, intent vectors, processes, or instructions integrable with physical or digital systems.

04: AUDIT

Relevant events are available for technical review, later validation, system improvement, or controlled documentation.
